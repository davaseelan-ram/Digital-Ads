Association of extended anticoagulation therapy with risk of recurrent VTE and bleeding rates in cancer-associated VTE
Moik F, et al. J Thromb Haemost. 2022;20(3):619–634.
•
 
KEY TAKEAWAY

Evidence on recurrence and bleeding risk between 6 and 12 months post index cancer-associated VTE showed:
• A heterogeneous risk of VTE recurrence (1%–12%)
• Highest risk being in patients discontinuing anticoagulation (13%–15%)
• An ongoing acceptable safety profile of extended anticoagulation as indicated by low bleeding rates (2%–5%)

The study findings support current guideline recommendations to extend anticoagulation beyond 6 months in patients with ongoing active cancer, based on an individualized risk–benefit evaluation.
WHY THIS MATTERS
Management of VTE in patients with cancer is challenging due to:
• 2-fold higher risk of major bleeding (MB)
• 3-to 7-fold higher risk of VTE recurrence during anticoagulation treatment vs non-cancer patients with VTE
In cancer-associated VTE, guidelines recommend at least 3–6 months of therapeutic anticoagulation and extended therapy in patients with active cancer or based on individual risk assessment.
• However, data on extended treatment beyond 6 months sare sparse.

This systematic review comprehensively summarizes the risks of recurrent VTE and bleeding between 6 and 12 months in patients with cancer-associated VTE based on available literature.
STUDY DESIGN

Databases screened: Medline, Embase, CENTRAL
INCLUSION CRITERIA
(a) Studies reporting results of clinical trials (controlled/single arm) or cohort studies (prospective/retrospective)
(b) Patients ≥18 years of age with active cancer
(c) Diagnosis of qualifying VTE including PE and/or DVT, either symptomatic or incidental/asymptomatic, and could include SVT, CVT, or CRT
(d) Follow-up and reporting of outcomes specifically between 6 and 12 months after the index VTE event
STUDY OUTCOMES
• Primary outcome events:
(a) Rate of recurrent VTE, including symptomatic, incidental, or fatal PE, DVT (including CRT), SVT, or CVT
(b) Rate of MB
• Secondary outcome events:
(a) Rate of clinically relevant non-major bleeding (CRNMB)
(b) All-cause mortality
KEY RESULTS
A total of 11 studies met the eligibility criteria.
• Studies varied in study-design, inclusion and exclusion criteria, and patient-characteristics.*
• Overall patients: 3,019 (with cancer-associated VTE at 6 months post index VTE)
RATES OF RECURRENT VTE AND BLEEDING BETWEEN 6 AND 12 MONTHS
AFTER INDEX DATE
• Highly variable VTE recurrence rates: Between 1.1% and 12.0% (I2 = 88%)
• MB rates: 1.7%–4.8% (I2 = 30%)
• CRNMB rates: 2.0%–4.8% (I2 = 64%)

LIMITATIONS
•

An aggregated quantitative estimate of VTE recurrence and bleeding rates could not be provided due to heterogeneity between identified studies.
• Study designs did not allow aggregated comparative analysis between different anticoagulation management strategies.
• Findings couldn’t be extrapolated to timeframes other than 6–12-month post index VTE.
• Most studies had non-randomized design, subgroup comparisons of anticoagulation treatments were infeasible due to underlying indication bias.
* Based on observed heterogeneity in studies, quantitative estimate of outcome rates was not calculated in the framework of meta-analysis
† Based on observed heterogeneity in studies, quantitative estimate of outcome rates was not calculated in the framework of meta-analysis
